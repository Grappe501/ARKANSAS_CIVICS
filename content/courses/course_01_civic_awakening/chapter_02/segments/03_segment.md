# Neighborhood Power

## Book Narrative

Before laws are written and votes are cast, ideas circulate through communities.

Neighbors talk.

Churches gather.

Civic groups meet.

Parents share concerns about schools.

These conversations may feel informal, but they often represent the first stage of civic action.

Movements rarely begin inside government buildings.

They begin in relationships.

## Course Activity

Learners identify local relationship networks.

## Workbook Exercise

List three places where community conversations happen.

## Workshop Activity

Participants map informal civic networks.

## Facilitator Notes

Show how social networks precede formal politics.

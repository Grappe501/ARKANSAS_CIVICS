# Power Is Not Where Most People Think

## Book Narrative

When most people think about political power, they imagine Washington.

The White House.

Congress.

Cable news debates.

But for most Arkansans, the decisions that shape daily life are not made in Washington at all.

They are made much closer to home.

A zoning vote at city hall.

A school board decision about curriculum.

A county quorum court vote about roads or budgets.

These decisions rarely make national headlines, yet they shape communities every single day.

Understanding civic life in Arkansas begins with understanding this simple truth.

Power is often local.

## Course Activity

Learners list three local decisions that affect their daily life.

## Workbook Exercise

Write down three local institutions that affect your life more directly than the federal government.

## Workshop Activity

Group discussion: where does real power operate in your town?

## Facilitator Notes

Shift the reader’s mental map away from national politics.

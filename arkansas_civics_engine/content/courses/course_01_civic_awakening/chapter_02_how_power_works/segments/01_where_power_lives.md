## Canonical Body

Many people assume political power lives mostly in Washington or at the Arkansas Capitol. In reality, power in Arkansas is distributed across multiple layers: neighborhood networks, local boards, city councils, county quorum courts, school districts, state agencies, courts, parties, and ballot processes.

If people do not know where power lives, they cannot know where to intervene.

## Workbook Prompt

Pick one local issue and list every institution that touches it from neighborhood to state level.

## Workshop Activity

Use a flip chart to map an issue upward from personal experience to neighborhood, local government, state government, direct democracy, and the courts.

## Facilitator Notes

Participants often jump too quickly to the governor or legislature. Keep them grounded in local structures first.

## Course Interaction

Interactive prompt: A school transportation problem appears in your city. Which institutions might influence the outcome before the issue reaches the state level?

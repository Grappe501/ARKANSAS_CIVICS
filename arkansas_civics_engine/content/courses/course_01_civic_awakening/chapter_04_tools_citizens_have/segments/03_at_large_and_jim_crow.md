## Canonical Body

At-large systems were often defended as simple and efficient, but in many places across the South they also functioned to dilute Black voting strength and leave minority communities without representation. This history matters because it teaches a larger lesson: procedures can look neutral while producing exclusionary outcomes.

## Workbook Prompt

What does fair representation require beyond the simple act of voting?

## Workshop Activity

Present a hypothetical city with a 40 percent minority community and a five-seat at-large council. Ask participants to compare representation outcomes under at-large and district systems.

## Facilitator Notes

Make clear that procedural design is never merely technical. It is part of the architecture of power.

## Course Interaction

Case prompt: Why might a community challenge an at-large election system under voting rights law?

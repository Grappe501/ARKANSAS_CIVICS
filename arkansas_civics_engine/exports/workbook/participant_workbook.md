# Arkansas Civics Workbook


# Civic Awakening


## Pulling Up More Chairs


### Name the Room Where Decisions Happen in Your Community
Write down one room in your community where important decisions are made. Who usually sits at the table? Who is usually absent?


### What Happens When People Stop Showing Up?
List three local decisions that affect your daily life. Were you or anyone you know in the room when those decisions were discussed?


### Why Do People Walk Away?
What are the top three reasons people in your community have stepped back from civic life?


### What Chair Could You Pull Up?
Name one civic space you could enter this month. Then name one table you could help create if the formal room still feels closed.


## How Power Actually Works


### Trace a Decision Through the Power Map
Pick one local issue and list every institution that touches it from neighborhood to state level.


### Your Local Institutions
List the city, county, and school entities that affect your household most directly.


### What Would You Put Before the Voters?
If you could place one issue before the people of Arkansas, what would it be and why?


### Plot Your Entry Point
Where do you naturally enter the map: neighborhood, local institution, statewide issue, or public narrative?


## The Arkansas Civic Story


### What Economic Pain Is Shared in Your Community?
What shared pain shows up in your county today—wages, hospital access, housing, transportation, school strain, or something else?


### What Does Backlash Look Like Today?
List three modern forms of backlash that can meet organizing even when violence is absent: legal barriers, economic pressure, narrative attacks, procedural sabotage, social intimidation.


### What Would Collective Power Look Like Today?
What issue in your community would require people to act together, not just complain separately?


### What Did Recent Petition Politics Reveal?
What did the recent petition wave teach ordinary Arkansans about power, process, and resistance?


## The Tools Citizens Already Have


### Initiative or Referendum?
Write one example of an issue better suited to an initiative and one better suited to a referendum.


### Which Voting System Produces Which Outcome?
Think of one election you know well. How might the result change under a different voting system?


### What Does Representation Mean?
What does fair representation require beyond the simple act of voting?


### Which Tool Matches Your Issue?
Choose one issue you care about. Which tool fits it best: relationship-building, local board advocacy, direct democracy, public storytelling, or coalition organizing?

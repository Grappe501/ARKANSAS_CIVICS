## Canonical Body

The answer to civic withdrawal is not louder performance. It is not another stage. It is not another argument where neighbors become enemies before they become listeners.

The first answer is simpler.

Pull up more chairs.

That means creating spaces where ordinary Arkansans can enter the conversation without already knowing the rules, the acronyms, the procedures, or the power players. It means making civic life feel human again—local, relational, understandable, and open.

A community changes when more people believe there is a place for them in the room.

## Workbook Prompt

Name one civic space you could enter this month. Then name one table you could help create if the formal room still feels closed.

## Workshop Activity

Closing round: Each person names one chair they are willing to pull up—for themselves, for a neighbor, or for a community voice that is usually missing.

## Facilitator Notes

The emotional move here is from diagnosis to possibility.

## Course Interaction

Reflection prompt: What is one small act of showing up that would change how you see yourself in civic life?

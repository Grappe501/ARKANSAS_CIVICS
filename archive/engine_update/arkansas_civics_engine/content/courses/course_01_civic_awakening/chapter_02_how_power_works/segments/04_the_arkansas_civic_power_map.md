## Canonical Body

The Arkansas Civic Power Map connects neighborhood conversations, civic movements, local government, state institutions, direct democracy, courts, and public narrative. It reminds readers that civic action is not random. It moves through identifiable layers of influence.

## Workbook Prompt

Where do you naturally enter the map: neighborhood, local institution, statewide issue, or public narrative?

## Workshop Activity

Display the map and ask participants to place sticky notes where they currently engage and where they want to engage next.

## Facilitator Notes

Return to this map often. It is one of the main memory devices in the suite.

## Course Interaction

Graphic interpretation prompt: If a movement starts with church basement conversations and ends at the Supreme Court, what stops did it likely make in between?

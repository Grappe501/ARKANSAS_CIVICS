## Canonical Body

The Elaine Massacre belongs in Arkansas civic history not only as a racial atrocity, but as a warning about what can happen when marginalized people organize in ways that threaten entrenched economic and political power. Black sharecroppers trying to demand fair payment were met with terror, mass killing, arrests, and legal abuse.

This history deepens the movement framework. Organizing does not always meet polite disagreement. Sometimes it meets repression.

## Workbook Prompt

List three modern forms of backlash that can meet organizing even when violence is absent: legal barriers, economic pressure, narrative attacks, procedural sabotage, social intimidation.

## Workshop Activity

Facilitated discussion: What can modern organizers learn from movements that faced backlash without romanticizing the harm they endured?

## Facilitator Notes

Ground the discussion in dignity, grief, and structural analysis. This is not a motivational anecdote.

## Course Interaction

Historical reflection: Why is it important for civic learners to understand not only how movements grow, but how power pushes back?

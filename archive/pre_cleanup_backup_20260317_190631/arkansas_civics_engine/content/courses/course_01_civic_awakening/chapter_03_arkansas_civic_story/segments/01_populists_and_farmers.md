## Canonical Body

Arkansas civic history did not begin with modern campaigns. Farmers, laborers, and local communities organized long before social media or consultants. Again and again, economic pain forced people to ask whether power was serving them or using them.

The lesson is simple: shared pain often becomes the first stage of a movement.

## Workbook Prompt

What shared pain shows up in your county today—wages, hospital access, housing, transportation, school strain, or something else?

## Workshop Activity

Groups identify one local pain point and translate it into movement language.

## Facilitator Notes

Connect history to present conditions. Ask people what has changed and what has not.

## Course Interaction

Case prompt: A farming county faces debt, outmigration, and school cuts. What kinds of organizing might emerge first?

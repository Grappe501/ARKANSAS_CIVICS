## Canonical Body

Arkansas is unusual because citizens have constitutional tools of direct democracy. Through initiatives and referendums, voters can propose laws, propose constitutional amendments, and challenge legislation. That means the people have more than the vote for candidates—they also hold structural power over policy design.

That power matters most when formal institutions stop listening.

## Workbook Prompt

If you could place one issue before the people of Arkansas, what would it be and why?

## Workshop Activity

Small-group exercise: brainstorm one issue that belongs in the legislature, one that belongs in local government, and one that might justify direct democracy.

## Facilitator Notes

Do not oversimplify petition politics. Make clear that the tool exists, but access to the tool can be contested.

## Course Interaction

Knowledge check: What is the practical difference between voting for a candidate and using a ballot initiative?
